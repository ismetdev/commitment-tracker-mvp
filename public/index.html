How to Use

What this is

A private, local tool to track what you pay each month, keep month-by-month history, and pay down personal debts. All data stays on your computer.

The basic flow

  1. Add commitments — your recurring monthly costs (rent, gym, subscriptions).
  2. Add debts — money you owe, with a monthly payment amount.
  3. Generate a month — creates that month from your active commitments and unpaid debts.
  4. Mark each line paid as you pay it. Paid vs pending totals update live.

Commitments

  • Types: fixed (same each month), variable (changes), optional.
  • Editing a commitment only affects future generated months. Past months never change.
  • Deactivate one to stop it appearing in new months (you can reactivate anytime).

Debts

  • Each debt has an original amount, a current balance, and a monthly payment.
  • If the monthly payment is more than 0, the debt appears as a line in each new month.
  • Marking that debt line paid reduces the balance. Unmarking it puts the balance back.
  • The last payment is capped to what's left — you never overpay.
  • When a debt hits 0, it stops appearing in new months.

Partial & extra payments

  • Paid less than usual? Click the amount on the month line, change it, then mark it paid. The balance drops by that amount.
  • Paid extra, off-schedule? Open the debt and use “Record an extra payment”.

Dashboard & carry-forward

  • The dashboard shows your latest month, total owed, and expected monthly cost.
  • If an earlier month still has unpaid items, a yellow warning lists it. Click to open and settle it. Your newest month isn't flagged — that's the one you're working on.

Backups

  • On the Dashboard, Download backup (.sqlite) saves a full copy. Keep it somewhere safe.
  • To restore: stop the app, replace data.sqlite with your backup (renamed), restart.
  • Export CSV gives a readable copy for Excel — for viewing, not restoring.

Months

  • A month can't be generated twice.
  • Delete this month (in the Monthly view) removes a month so you can regenerate it. Any paid debt lines have their balances restored first, so nothing is lost.
